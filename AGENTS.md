# Tiny Secular Sacrament

A single physical pamphlet inspired by religious conversion tracts, but emptied of guilt, fear, and doctrine. Instead of asking the reader to believe in a god, it invites them to separate from the voice in their head and return to awareness.

The object should feel like a relic: small, sincere, strange, beautiful, and worth keeping. The parody lives in the format, not the writing. The text should be earnest, poetic, and freeing — never snarky, meme-y, or “AI wellness PDF.”

Core message:

> You are not your thoughts.  
> You are the awareness that notices them.

Influences include *The Untethered Soul*, *The Gifts of Imperfection*, *The Artist’s Way*, *Don’t Believe Everything You Think*, and attachment/inner-child work, but the pamphlet should use original language.

Vibe:
- secular devotional
- high-design tract
- pocket-sized sacrament
- tender, not cheesy
- reverent, not religious
- universal, not customized
- printed artifact first, website second

Avoid:
- generic wellness language
- parody that mocks believers
- generated/customized content
- self-help branding
- cheap irony

Goal: one canonical pamphlet that feels discovered, not produced.
